about myselt

Hi there! My name is Rohan. I am a developer, researcher, and designer who advocates for implementation and design of inclusive experiences.

I am a graduate student pursuing masters in Human-Computer Interaction at Rochester Institute of Technology in Rochester, NY and currently seeking full-time opportunities starting June/August 2019. I also hold masters in Computer Science and have experience through multiple co-ops working closely with developers and designers to create customer-facing and enterprise products that adhere to UX principles.

I am currently working on a research project under the supervision of Dr. Kristen Shinohara, Dr. Yasmine El-Glaly, and Dr. Catherine Baker that aims to investigate the current state and perception of accessibility amongst front-end Technology Professionals. I am also a participant of healthcare Hackathon - MIT Hacking Medicine - for the past three years. In 2017, my team won the “Best Epilepsy Hack” award for proposing a novel approach to provide accurate, verified information about epilepsy for newly diagnosed patients and their family.

At RIT, I oversee the operations of on-campus food pantry - RIT FoodShare - as a coordinator that assists students experiencing food insecurity. Besides academics, I engage myself towards the betterment of the local community through volunteering to eliminate food waste and food insecurity; and promoting sustainability and reuse. Occasionally, I enjoy playing tennis, exploring new hiking trails, practice yoga and meditation.
